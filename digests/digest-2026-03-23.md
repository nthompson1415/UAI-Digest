**2. LAAF: Automated Framework for Red-Teaming LLM Agents**
A new paper introduces LAAF, the first automated framework designed to detect Logic-layer Prompt Control Injection (LPCI) vulnerabilities in agentic Large Language Model (LLM) systems. This development is a significant advancement in AI security, addressing the urgent challenge of red-teaming these complex systems.
⚠️ [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.17239) · 2026-03-19

**3. Bootstrapping Coding Agents: Specification as Program**
This research demonstrates a remarkable bootstrapping capability in coding agents, where a new agent can be generated from a specification. This mirrors classical compiler construction and represents a significant step towards self-improving AI systems with broad implications for AI development.
⚠️ [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.17399) · 2026-03-19

**4. Mixture-of-Depths Attention (MoDA) for Efficient LLMs**
Researchers have released a paper on Mixture-of-Depths Attention (MoDA), a hardware-aware implementation for efficient LLMs. Experiments show MoDA consistently outperforms baselines, improving performance on downstream tasks with a negligible increase in computational overhead, suggesting it's a promising primitive for depth scaling in AI models.
⚠️ [GitHub](https://github.com/hustvl/MoDA) · 2026-03-17

**5. Why AI Systems Don't Learn and What to Do About It**
This paper critically examines the limitations of current AI models in achieving autonomous learning and proposes a novel, biologically inspired architectural framework. The research aims to advance the field towards more general and adaptive intelligence by addressing fundamental challenges in AI learning.
⚠️ [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.15381) · 2026-03-17
