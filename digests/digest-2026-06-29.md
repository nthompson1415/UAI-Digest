A new algorithm called Reversal Q-Learning (RQL) has been introduced for training AI agents using existing data. It cleverly treats each step of a generative process as a separate action, overcoming instability issues common in training complex models. Experiments show RQL significantly outperforms other methods in simulated robotic tasks.

## ⚖️ Policy & Regulation

**1. New Executive Order Focuses on AI Cybersecurity and National Security**
On June 2, 2026, President Trump signed an executive order titled "Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security." This order establishes a voluntary framework for developers of advanced AI models to share them with the federal government for cybersecurity and national security assessments up to 30 days before public release. It also directs agencies to enhance federal cyber defenses and create an AI cybersecurity clearinghouse. This represents a shift from previous orders that focused on deregulation, indicating a growing recognition of AI's national security implications.
⚠️ [The White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/briefing-room/presidential-actions/2026/06/02/promoting-advanced-artificial-intelligence-innovation-and-security/) · 2026-06-02

**2. GSA Proposes New Rules for Safeguarding Government Data in Large Language Models**
The U.S. General Services Administration (GSA) has proposed a new rule that would significantly revise requirements for safeguarding government data processed by large language models (LLMs). The proposed rule, published on June 29, 2026, includes strict government data ownership and use restrictions, "eyes off" data handling, U.S. jurisdictional controls, and robust incident reporting. It also introduces an "Unbiased AI Principles" framework. This rule applies to GSA's governmentwide contracts and could lead to suspension of AI system use or termination for cause if not complied with.
⚠️ [DLA Piper](https://www.dlapiper.com/en/us/insights/publications/2026/06/gsa-proposes-sweeping-ai-data-safeguarding-rules-for-llm-contractors/) · 2026-06-29

**3. US Government Establishes Framework for AI Model Cybersecurity Review**
On June 2, 2026, President Trump signed an executive order establishing a voluntary framework for the government review of advanced AI models, referred to as "covered frontier" models. This order directs agencies to bolster federal cyber defenses and create an AI cybersecurity clearinghouse. While participation is voluntary, the increased federal attention to the cyber capabilities of these advanced AI systems may set new expectations for engagement between developers and national security agencies.
⚠️ [Insights](https://www.insights.com/insights/articles/executive-order-on-artificial-intelligence-expands-cybersecurity-federal-oversight/) · 2026-06-16

**4. State Legislatures Continue to Introduce AI-Related Bills**
In the 2025 legislative session, all 50 states, Puerto Rico, the Virgin Islands, and Washington, D.C., introduced AI-related legislation, with 38 states enacting around 100 measures. Examples include Arkansas clarifying ownership of AI-generated content and Montana's "Right to Compute" law setting risk management policy requirements for AI-controlled critical infrastructure. New York enacted a law requiring state agencies to publish information about their automated decision-making tools and strengthening worker protections related to AI use.
⚠️ [National Conference of State Legislatures](https://www.ncsl.org/research/technology/artificial-intelligence-2025-legislation.aspx) · 2026-06-16

**5. Executive Order Aims to Pre-empt State AI Regulations**
In December 2025, President Trump issued an executive order aiming to establish a uniform federal policy framework for AI and pre-empt conflicting state laws. The order directed the Attorney General to form a task force to litigate against state AI laws and empowered the Secretary of Commerce to review "onerous" state AI laws. It also instructed federal agencies to evaluate their grant programs to ensure states are not enacting AI laws that contradict federal policy.
